Introduction

Driving a scooter on the university of Florida campus can be one of your happiest experiences during your time as a student. The hobby has become so popular that in 2013, scooter drivers made up approximately 10% of all drivers who park on UF campus (UF, Campus Master Plan 2015-2025). This web page is meant to show the students and residents of Gainesville the proper riding attire to safely scoot around town.


When you drive a scooter or motorcycle, you sacrifice safety features in cars, such as a hard-shell exterior, seat belts, and airbags to name a few. To make up for the risks driving a two-wheel vehicle imposes, you must always wear protective gear, such as gloves, a jacket, and a sturdy pair of pants that reaches your ankles. While this may seem like a burden at times, it would be much more favorable than the road rash you’ll earn by wearing a tank top and shorts. Always remember to wear clothing that will protect if need to lay your bike down while driving.

Using a helmet is essential to riding any two-wheeled vehicle. The primary cause of death on a scooter is traumatic brain injury. This happens when your head impacts the pavement at a high speed. Often, the helmet ratings for scooters and motorcyclists can be confusing when looking to purchase. However, helmets are rated on a variety of factors that determine their effeciveness and certification rating (such as, DOT, ECE 22.05, and SNELL). Just wearing a helmet in a collision can reduce your chances of death by 40% (Injury Epidemiology, 2016). Why take the chance?

When riding a scooter, gloves should be worn to prevent serious injuries to the hands. In an accident, what's the first body part to make contact with the pavement? The hands; its instinctive to brace yourself upon impact. Additionally, gloves keep the hands warm in the winter. This prevents shaking and over-gripping the handlebars. Therefore, you should always wear your gloves when you scoot.

You must always wear protective, close-toed shoes or boots that cover the ankle when riding. Boots are integral when riding in case the vehicle falls or gets knocked-down. Also, in an emergency, the feet serve as last resort brakes. Ultimately, the wrong footwear could be fatal.
